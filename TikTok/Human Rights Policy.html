Technology is an essential gateway to the exercise of human rights. TikTok touches more than 1 billion people across the globe. We are committed to respecting the human rights of all people impacted by our platform, regardless of their ethnicity, orientation, background, or life experience. As a global entertainment company, TikTok has a responsibility to ensure that our community is treated with fundamental dignity and respect on our platform.


Our commitment to human rights

Responsibility for upholding human rights is shared; while governments have a duty to protect human rights, TikTok and other businesses also have a responsibility to respect those human rights. Respecting human rights is essential for TikTok to build and sustain trust among our employees, users, creators, advertisers, and others who are part of our community and who we engage with as a business.

Our philosophy is informed by, and we are committed to, key several international human rights frameworks, including (1) the International Bill of Human Rights (which includes the Universal Declaration of Human Rights [UDHR], the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights [ICCPR], and the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights [ICESCR]), (2) the International Labour Organization (ILO) Declaration on Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work, (3) the Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC), and (4) the United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs).

As part of our commitment, we strive to respect human rights throughout our business and in our business relationships while also complying with applicable laws and regulations intended to promote human rights where we conduct business globally. We endeavor to continuously evaluate our operations to identify, assess, and address salient human rights risks, engage key rights holders, and prioritize areas where we have the greatest opportunity to have a positive impact.

Operating as a global company obliges us to recognize and abide by local laws in the countries where we operate. In some situations, local laws may conflict with international human rights standards. When presented with these cases, our commitment to human rights remains and we seek to uphold our users’ rights to free expression and privacy. To do this, we assess the validity of the government request that is in conflict, and interpret it as narrowly as possible. In cases where we believe this is insufficient to respect our users’ rights, we will explore legal means to push back against requests that may undermine international law and international human rights standards.


Implementing our commitments

Oversight

Key leaders across TikTok help provide oversight on human rights related actions and decisions. Our cross-functional human rights working group coordinates and collaborates on human rights initiatives.


Policies & frameworks

Our commitments to human rights are embodied within the overarching policies and frameworks governing our day to day work. Some notable examples include (but are not limited to):

  • Community Guidelines: These guidelines include the rules and standards for using TikTok which apply to everyone and everything on our platform. Our community principles are the foundation of our Community Guidelines and are grounded in human rights standards. These principles enable the development and enforcement of our rules in a way that balances freedom of expression with harm prevention, embraces human dignity, and ensures our actions are fair.
  • Guidelines on Handling Government Removal Requests & Law Enforcement Guidelines: These guidelines outline our practices governing content takedowns and user data disclosure based on requests from governments and law enforcement agencies. The guidelines are consistently applied across regions and have been developed with the freedom of expression and privacy of our users in mind.
  • Privacy Policies: Our privacy policies provide details about what information we collect and how we use and share this information. Our community members can submit a request to exercise their privacy rights under applicable laws. Our policies are in line with our commitment to the International Bill of Human Rights and respect the right to privacy and remedy contained within.
  • Advertising Policies: TikTok’s advertising policies specify the types of products or services which can be advertised on our platform. These policies have been built with the protection of our users in mind, especially those who may be most vulnerable. TikTok’s Anti-Discrimination Ad Policy prohibits advertisers from using our ads products to discriminate against people due to wrongful ad targeting.
  • Codes of Conduct: Our codes of conduct, such as the TikTok Shop Business Partner Code of Conduct, set standards and responsibilities for business partners in the areas of compliance, business ethics, labor and human rights, health and safety, and environment.
  • Transparency Reporting: Our transparency reports provide information about our Community Guidelines enforcement, government content removal requests, law enforcement requests for user information, intellectual property removal requests, disrupted covert influence operations, child sexual exploitation and abuse detection and reporting, and TikTok Shop safety and intellectual property rights.


Key Priorities

  • Promoting youth safety and well-being: We are deeply committed to preserving the rights of people under the age of 18 (we refer to them as "youth" or "young people"). We do not allow content that may put young people at risk of psychological, physical, or developmental harm. Some examples of our work to prevent these types of harms include setting minimum age requirements for access to certain product features, using restrictive default privacy settings, and setting content levels to restrict content that may not be suitable for those under 18. We report incidents of youth sexual abuse and exploitation to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC). We also report to relevant law enforcement authorities when there is a specific, credible, and imminent threat to human life or serious physical injury. In addition, we have many resources to support young people. These can be found in our Safety Center, including the dedicated Teen Safety Center, Help Center, and Privacy Center. We also aim to provide parents and guardians with resources they can use to have conversations with their family about digital safety and decide the most comfortable experience for them, such as the Guardian’s Guide and Family Pairing features.
  • Advancing inclusion: TikTok believes in creating an equitable experience for all community members. TikTok's inclusion and belonging guide contains a summary of all tools and features available to keep community members safe. This includes a range of safety features, such as confidential ways to report potentially violative content and accounts, comment filtering tools and privacy settings to choose who can engage with their content. We also offer new tools to promote kindness and safety, including ways to customize your For You feed with keyword filters and Refresh. TikTok's global campaigns have furthered the participation of our community members in various aspects of local and global cultural life. Examples include our work to support indigenous creators and global pride campaigns.
  • Preserving integrity and authenticity: TikTok's suite of integrity and authenticity policies, tools, and product solutions enable our commitment to the right to information. These tackle a vast range of issues such as protecting the integrity of elections, combating harmful misinformation, countering influence operations, labelling state-affiliated media entities, strengthening security and privacy and supporting responsible, transparent AI-generated content. They are supplemented by our global fact-checking program and partnerships with organizations such as the WHO, Democracy Works, and Verified for Climate to ensure our community members have access to accurate information about important topics such as elections and mental health.
  • Supporting the economic empowerment of small and medium-sized businesses (SMB's): TikTok is committed to empowering SMBs, entrepreneurs, and creators to succeed and grow their businesses on TikTok including via TikTok Shop. TikTok has signed the World Economic Forum Social Innovators Pledge demonstrating our support for financing social innovation. Examples of our work include the SOAR Together Program focused on supporting and empowering women-owned and LGBTQIA+ businesses.


Human rights risk identification & mitigation

We strive to proactively identify and mitigate human rights risks across our business. Some actions include:

  • Training and capacity building: trainings are provided for our employees and business partners, which cover topics related to human rights.
  • Product development and deployment: safety, privacy, and legal reviews are conducted in product development and deployment phases to mitigate potential risks.
  • Community Guidelines development and updates: Community Guidelines are developed and updated based on emerging risk areas identified via external consultation and community member reporting. This includes an examination of moderation and account related decisions which have been appealed by members should they believe these decisions have been incorrectly enforced. More information here.
  • High risk events: we have several standard operating procedures to monitor and address high risk events around the world, such as elections, civic unrest, conflicts, and humanitarian crises. Measures include (but are not limited to) enhancing risk monitoring capacity, in app safety features and notifications, and being transparent about the measures and steps we take during these critical times.


Stakeholder engagement

We engage with external experts to inform us of ongoing and upcoming human rights risks around the world. This includes engaging members of affected communities or larger organizations representing their interests.

Our proactive engagement includes human rights organizations, independent experts and members of our Advisory Councils. We work collaboratively with these experts to inform and strengthen our policies, product features, and safety processes. In addition, through our Youth Council we actively engage young people, to hear their insights and recommendations, and to help us better understand and respond to their safety and well-being needs on our platform.

Our Community Partner Channel, which includes safety and civil society organizations with a broad range of expertise across content safety and youth safety issues, can report potentially violative content to us directly for review. These partners supplement our content moderation practices by identifying and sharing locally nuanced risks.

Our ongoing memberships in industry groups allow us to engage with our peers on current and upcoming trends. For example, we are members of the WeProtect Global Alliance, Trust and Safety Professional Association (TSPA), Tech Against Terrorism, Business for Social Responsibility, the Tech Coalition, Digital Trust & Safety Partnership, Tech Against Trafficking, and launch partners for Partnership on AI's Framework Responsible Practices for Synthetic Media.


Transparency

Transparency reporting and centers

Our transparency reports provide information about our Community Guidelines enforcement, government content removal requests, law enforcement requests for user information, intellectual property removal requests, disrupted covert influence operations, child sexual exploitation and abuse detection and reporting, and TikTok Shop safety and intellectual property rights.

Our transparency center houses information about how we take steps to keep our users safe via our content moderation processes. This includes how we use automated technology to enforce our Community Guidelines, how our external partners flag potentially violative content, and the actions we take to support the well-being of our moderators.

In addition, we share information about how our For You feed works, including the signals used to recommend content and the steps we take to prioritize safety when curating content. Lastly, our center showcases our work to facilitate independent research about our platform through our Research Tools, including our Research API and Virtual Compute Environment. Through our Tools, qualifying researchers in the U.S. and Europe can apply to study public data about TikTok content and accounts. We're working to provide increased access to Research Tools in the future. We also provide access to a Commercial Content Library and the Commercial Content API for those interested in studying commercial content on TikTok. These APIs are made available globally and will include ads data from EU to begin with, while we build capacity to expand to various commercial and regional data sets.

To enhance transparency and increase external accountability, we have built Transparency and Accountability Centers (TACs) in Dublin, Los Angeles, Singapore and Washington, D.C. to provide lawmakers and other external stakeholders a chance to review and understand our algorithmic and content moderation practices.


Sharing the results of our human rights impact assessments

We partnered with Article One, a business and human rights consultancy, to conduct a human rights impact assessment (HRIA) of our trust and safety operations, in line with the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights. This assessment identified the following key areas of focus for our human rights strategy within Trust and Safety:

  • Developing a formalized approach to managing human rights across Trust and Safety.
  • Aligning operational approaches with relevant human rights standards. This includes applying the ICCPR three-part test which seeks to expand freedom of expression and the Rabat plan which outlines the conditions for limiting speech which could incite hatred to our content moderation policies.
  • Integrating human rights into policy areas, including as it relates to protection for public figures and human rights defenders.
  • Enhancing language coverage to ensure that content moderation is uniformly resourced around the world.
  • Expanding protections of content moderators by supporting technological innovations to address risks to moderators and engaging in cross-industry collaboration on potential solutions.


TikTok has taken a number of steps to align with the recommendations from the assessment. Some examples include:

  • Developing a framework to assess country risk based on short and long term risks such as conflict and governance. This has been integrated into TikTok's risk monitoring exercises and market resource planning.
  • Developing a policy assessment review framework that considers ICCPR as well as other standards. This assessment is conducted for every Community Guideline that is created or revamped.
  • Incorporating the Rabat Plan's 6 part test in Trust and Safety's harm severity principles and corresponding harm severity rankings of all Community Guidelines.
  • Engaging with organizations specializing in human rights and adding groups representing the interests of human rights defenders to our Community Partner Channel.
  • Expanding global fact-checking coverage to 50 languages and 100 markets across the world.
  • Partnering with the Trust & Safety Foundation (TSF) and engaging in its cross-industry group of research experts on Trust & Safety Wellbeing. We identify risks and controls through collaborative research for all Trust & Safety employees.
  • Investing in automated moderation technologies and tooling features to reduce exposure to harmful content. This includes specialized queues which research has shown can reduce the startle response that is associated with adverse stress reactions when viewing harmful content.
