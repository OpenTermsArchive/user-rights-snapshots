Lead ads are not available to everyone on Pinterest just yet. If you’re interested in trying them out, reach out to your account manager.

Lead ads help you reach people who are actively looking for, and interested in your goods and services. People on Pinterest share their information with you by filling out a form within your Lead ad, without having to leave the Pinterest app or website. You’re in control of what text appears on the lead form’s description, questions and confirmation sections. Once people have filled out the form, you can download the responses for up to 30 days via the Pinterest Ads Manager or Pinterest API, or by using our integration with Zapier, Salesforce, or Salesforce Sales Cloud.

You’ll set up your conversions campaign first, then create a Lead ad with corresponding lead form made up of the following components:

  • Call to action: The button someone clicks to fill out the form, such as “Sign up”. You’ll select your preferred call to action from a range of options.
  • Privacy policy link: A link to your company’s privacy policy so people know how their information will be used.
  • (Optional) Additional disclosure language: Additional legal language you can add to the lead form’s existing legal language.
  • Questions: Mandatory prompts people respond to with their information, such as “Email”. You’ll select your preferred questions from a range of options or write your own.
  • Completion message: The customized message people see once they’ve filled out and submitted the form.

    Create a Lead ad

    Download your leads

    Leads can be downloaded from the Ads Manager reporting page. You can set the date range at the top of the Reporting page to a maximum of 30 days.

    Note: Lead ad data will be deleted 30 days after the lead has been submitted and cannot be retrieved.

    If you’re accessing a Pinterest business account as an employee, you must have admin level access to be able to download and manage lead data.

    First, you’ll create a lead column, as well as a cost per lead column on your reporting table so you can access this data.

  • Log in to your Pinterest business account
  • Click  the hamburger icon  at the top-left of the page
  • Under Manage campaigns, click Campaign Manager
  • At the top of the table, click Edit Columns
  • Scroll down to Engagement
  • Select Lead and Cost per lead
  • Click Apply
    • Optional: Click Save table to save this filter in your table for future use
    •

    Once you’ve created these columns, you can download your leads.

  • Log in to your Pinterest business account
  • Click  the hamburger icon  at the top-left of the page
  • Under Manage campaigns, click Campaign Manager
  • Click the Ads tab at the top of your table
  • Next to Targeting breakdown, click Table, and then select Lead Generation
  • In the Leads column in the table, click Download
  • Click Download again to confirm

    Sync Lead ads with Salesforce Marketing Cloud

    Integrate your Pinterest Lead ads with Salesforce Marketing Cloud (SFMC) to automatically sync your Pinterest leads between both accounts. 

    Prerequisites

    Before you begin the integration process, you’ll need:

  • An account on Salesforce Marketing Cloud Engagement
  • At least one lead ad created on Pinterest
  • Admin/Owner access to the Pinterest ad account you’ll be integrating.
    •  Setup
  • Log in to your Salesforce Marketing Cloud account
  • Go to the Install Page
  • Click the Install button in the right-hand corner. Refresh the page when the install is complete.
  • In the Salesforce Marketing Cloud, click AppExchange in the navigation bar
  • Under AppExchange, select Pinterest Lead Ads
  • Click Connect and log in using your Pinterest credentials
  • Select the Pinterest ad account you’d like to use and click Next
  • Choose the lead form you’d like to use and click Next
  • Select the Subscriber Key to connect the lead form to a new data extension within Salesforce Marketing Cloud.
  • Click Submit and Authorize to complete the integration process.
  • Send test data (Optional)
    1. Click on the Send test data button
    2. Go to the Data Extension associated with the integration.
    3. Verify that the test record has been correctly marked with TEST_API in the LEAD_ID column.
    •  Deleting connections and extension

    If you delete your SFMC connection after integrating with Pinterest then new leads won't sync automatically and the created data extension won't be deleted. You can remove the associated data extension manually in SFMC.

    If you delete the data extension Pinterest won't be able to insert lead data records.

    Troubleshooting

    If you can’t install the package:

  • Identify Your Website Stack:
    • In Salesforce Marketing Cloud, determine your website stack (ex. S4, S6, S7, S10, S11, S12, S13, S50, S51) based on your account URL.
    • For additional assistance, refer to the Salesforce Help Center for guidance on identifying the correct stack.
  • Modify Installation URL:
    • Replace the "s13" in the following URL with your identified website stack: 
      https://mc.s13.exacttarget.com/cloud/#app/Administration/InstalledPackages/6105e5dc-70ad-4996-b8b9-0765b298753c/install
