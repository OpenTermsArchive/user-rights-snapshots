About ads for housing, employment or financial products and services

As part of our ongoing efforts to promote fairness on our advertising platform, we have introduced a new "Financial products and services" Special Ad Category for advertisers promoting financial products and services. Starting from 21 January 2025, using this category is required for financial products and services campaigns for advertisers based in the United States or showing ads to audiences in the United States. Ads may be rejected if an appropriate category is not chosen. Learn more about the update here.

To help prevent discrimination, our Discriminatory Practices advertising policy has special requirements for ads related to housing, employment or financial products and services.

Note: The Credit special ad category has been replaced by the Financial products and services category. This video covers the credit components that are in scope for our requirements. Additionally, this category has expanded to include financial products and services, which you can learn more about here.

When you run financial products and services ads, you may need to choose Special Ad Category during the creation of your campaign. You'll need to do this if:

  • You're an advertiser in the United States
  • Your audience is in the United States
  • You're running ads for credit opportunities in the US, Canada or certain parts of Europe, or reaching audiences in these regions

Example ads for housing, employment and financial products and services

Note: Starting on 21 January 2025, use of the financial products and services Special Ad Category is required for advertisers based in the United States or showing financial products and services campaigns to audiences in the United States. Ads may be rejected if the advertiser does not choose an appropriate Special Ad Category. Our policy for credit ads (a subset of financial products and services) does not change, and will continue to apply to advertisers based in the US or showing ads to audiences in the US, Canada or certain parts of Europe.

If you're unsure whether your ad is covered under Meta's Discriminatory Practices policy for housing, employment or financial products and services ads, you can review examples of covered ads below.

Ads for financial products and services

Ads that promote or directly link to a financial product or service offer, including credit

Here are some examples of ads for credit, a subset of financial products and services, under our policies:

They can include offers for the following and more in the US, Canada or certain parts of Europe:

  • Credit cards
  • Brand ads for credit cards, regardless of offer
  • Loans, including car, mortgage, personal or business loans
  • Long-term financing
  • Debt recovery
  • Debt consolidation and relief

Additionally, in the US, they can also include other financial products and services like the following and more:

  • Banking services including current and savings accounts
  • Brand ads for financial products and services, regardless of offer
  • Prepaid cards
  • Investment services
  • Pension or retirement funds
  • Insurance products
  • Consumer payment services
  • 0% APR instalment payments, including buy now, pay later (BNPL) and in-house instalment payments

They don't include offers for the following:

  • Gift cards
  • Store rewards
  • Business-to-Business (B2B)-only financial products and services
  • Tax and accounting services

Ads for housing

Ads that promote or directly link to a housing opportunity or related service.

Here are some examples of ads for housing under our policies:

They can include offers for the following and more:

  • Sale, rental or temporary housing listings
  • Homeowners insurance offers, including as part of insurance bundles
  • Renters insurance offers, including as part of insurance bundles
  • Mortgage insurance offers, including as part of insurance bundles
  • Financing options, including mortgage loans
  • Home equity or appraisal services
  • Property and house hunting services
  • Aggregator services

They don't include offers for the following:

  • Hotels
  • Resorts
  • Retreats
  • Tips related to home ownership or home buying
  • Education for consumers or housing providers about their rights and responsibilities under fair housing laws

Ads for employment

Ads that promote or directly link to an employment opportunity.

Here are some examples of ads for employment under our policies:

They can include offers for the following and more:

  • Full-time job
  • Part-time job
  • Internship
  • Apprenticeships
  • Professional certification programmes
  • Job fair
  • Job board or aggregation services
  • Guaranteed interviews or career placement
  • Promotion of company perks alongside employment opportunities

They don't include offers for the following:

  • General advice on how to succeed at work unrelated to specific listings
  • General advice for career development unrelated to specific listings
  • Discussing a company without sharing employment opportunities or perks

Remember that the examples in the sections above aren't comprehensive and don't constitute legal advice.

Audience restrictions

When you choose a special ad category, we apply additional audience restrictions to your campaign. These restrictions are a preventative measure to protect people from discriminatory practices. Your audience options don't become limited to only people interested in housing, employment and financial products and services opportunities. Learn more about audience restrictions for employment, housing and financial products and services ads.

If you determine that your ad is for housing, employment or financial products and services, learn how to choose a Special Ad Category in Meta Ads Manager. Remember that choosing a Special Ad Category doesn't affect how long it takes for us to review your ads.

Learn more about how to troubleshoot a rejected ad. If you believe that your ad was rejected incorrectly, you can request a review in Business Support Home.
